Haiku
Description
A three-line poem of 5,7,5 syllables; haiku writers seek to capture a moment of perception. Haikus turn on strong natural images - using a word, called the kigo, that indicates the season - and relay intense emotions, often leading to spiritual insights. Contemporary haiku writers may drop the three-line requirement (often writing the poem as a single line) and/or the syllable count and/or the kigo.
