Insharika

Tanka (also called waka or uta)

Description

A traditional Japanese poem that is rendered in English in five lines of 5,7,5,7,7 syllables. Historically, tanks were combined with prose, linked together, and/or published to include exchanges of verse between poets. Subjects vary but often center on travel, love, and the seasons. As with haiku, in Japan today there are regular tanka competitions and a continuing appreciation of this form.
